WHAT'S GOOD

As the end of Puppet preludes, What's Good finally sees Igor entering the scene. In this track, we hear Tyler rapping like he did in his early projects. He's provocative, vulgar and sarcastic.

We can now understand the reason behind the name's choice for the Igor persona. Igor is the sarcastic and brute side of Tyler, but It is also the way he copes with pain, as he did with his earlier works. Just like Igor is deformed but he's also an assistant. I believe Igor is also used by the singer to put in frame his older homophobic lines, as a newly discovered bisexual. The end of the songs serves as an introduction for the final act of the love story, when Tyler needs to accept that his love is gone.
